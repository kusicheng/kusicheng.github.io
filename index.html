About

Hello! I’m Kevin, an inventor, ski enthusiast, and pianist combined into one. Though I place heavy emphasis on my academic goals, I’m likely to get carried away whenever skiing or instruments are mentioned. As a conversational person, I like casual and honest exchanges, where I can converse more about my interests in AI, skiing, or piano. Experimenting with new things is a hobby of mine, such as exploring GitHub for new resources - especially from Google, where I hope to find interesting or valuable insight through others’ tools.

Hobbies

Skiing: Yabuli

Known for the cold -30 °C (-22 °F) weather, Yabuli was the coldest ski resort I ever visited. Here, I was taught by a ski instructor for the last time. I visited this resort by train with 3 of the boys, and we all learned under the same instructor. Most memorably, after our instructor left, I would guide us down the black and double black runs to challenge our skills and test our endurance.

As my first in-china ski trip, this marked the beginning of my odessey with friends and students on journeys across the world.

Skiing: Hakuba

Happo One (pronounced “oh-neh”), an Olympic venue, was stacked with fresh powder after La Niña brought weeks of consecutive storms.

The day I brought the boys over, it was time to strap on and hit the slopes. I taught the amateur - Ryan - and I fixed the technique for the powder hound Minwu.


During this trip, I was able to apply my CSIA certification in real life, resulting in substantially more improvement in Ryan and Minwu compared to my first attempts.

Piano teaching

Teaching piano at SenDi was a novel experience. There, I took on the role of an assistant teacher, where I was in charge of preparing materials for hand practice and teaching them. This was a different experience than academic tutoring, as I often had to play and point to problems on the spot. Though tiring, I committed to this as I loved seeing others improve and have an "aha" moment, as it's rewarding to see my students improve step by step, often even faster than I did.

Miscellaneous Activities

National High School Game Academy at CMU, final game presentation: Cow Party Game.

This is a game my group and I made over the summer - a third person shooter fps that features 1v1 and large scale team battles. This was built from scratch over the span of 2 weeks, where I was the sole composer of all sound, integrated through FMOD and C# programming. My team’s artists built and engineered model and model dynamics, while our 3 programmers were in charge of game logic. This experience not only furthered my programming and musical composition, but also taught me how to bridge cultural gaps and facilitate teamwork in a diverse team. This experience allowed me to learn Jazz and a little bit of Wwise studio - a similar software to FMOD.


National High School Game Academy at CMU, first round game presentation: Nuclear Kong

At CMU, we also made an adaptation of a classic arcade game, one example being Asteroids. My group and I got the game Donkey Kong, and we adapted it by changing the setting to a nuclear power plant and adding a time dynamic through the rising nuclear waste water. The sounds for this game were extremely exaggerated and cartoonish, thus being a very fun endeavor. This is also when I first tried out 8-Bit music composition. I learned a lot of programming both in FMOD and in Unity editor itself to debug and faciliate the usage of sounds. Since I had a lot of previous experience in code, I helped other teams integrate and debug FMOD and Perforce.
