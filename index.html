About

Hello! I’m Kevin, an inventor, ski enthusiast, and pianist combined into one. Though I place heavy emphasis on my academic goals, I’m likely to get carried away whenever skiing or instruments are mentioned. As a conversational person, I like casual and honest exchanges, where I could converse more on my interests in AI, skiing, or piano. Experimenting with new things is a hobby of mine, such as exploring Github for new resources - especially from Google, where I hope to find interesting or valuable insight through others tools. 

Hobbies

Skiing: Yabuli

Known for the cold -30°C (-22°F) weather, Yabuli was the coldest ski resort I ever visited. Here, I was taught by a ski instructor for the last time. I visited this resort by train with 3 of the boys, and we all learned under the same instructor. Most memorably, After our instructor left, I would guide us down the black and double black runs to challenge our skills and test our endurance. 

As my first in-china ski trip, this marked the beginning of my odessey with friends and students on journeys across the world.

Skiing: Canada

I keep a photo diary for my trips in Canada. To the left is a picture of Blue Mountain last time I visited. Though we weren’t allowed to take photos while in training, I still photographed some images that captured the natural beauty of the landscape - one of the things I enjoy the most in skiing. After classes, I would arrange mock teaching sessions with other candidates to improve our teaching and hopefully pass together. 

Skiing: Hakuba

Happo One (pronounced “oh-neh”), an Olympic venue, was stacked with fresh powder after La Niña brought weeks of consecutive storms. 

The day I brought the boys over, it was time to strap on and hit the slopes. I taught the amateur - Ryan - and I fixed the technique for the powder hound Minwu. 


During this trip, I was able to apply my CSIA certification in real life, resulting in substantially more improvement in Ryan and Minwu compared to my first attempts.

Miscellaneous

National High School Game Academy at CMU, final game presentation: Cow Party Game.

This is a game my group and I made over the summer - a third person shooter fps that features 1v1 and large scale team battles. This was built from scratch over the span of 2 weeks, where I was the sole composer of all sound, integrated through FMOD and C# programming. My team’s artists built and engineered model and model dynamics, while our 3 programmers were in charge of game logic. This experience not only furthered my programming and musical composition, but also taught me how to bridge cultural gaps and facilitate teamwork in a diverse team.
